# Decision Report Writer

Use this skill to produce decision reports that leaders can inspect and challenge.

## Report Structure

Use this structure unless the user gives a stricter format:

1. Executive recommendation
2. Decision context
3. Evidence summary
4. Options considered
5. Key verified claims
6. Unsupported or contradicted claims
7. Risks and mitigations
8. Confidence and assumptions
9. Next actions

## Rules

- Cite evidence for every important factual claim.
- Do not hide uncertainty. Name weak evidence and missing documents.
- Separate observations from recommendations.
- Include confidence levels and why confidence is limited.
- Never upgrade an unsupported claim into a recommendation without labeling it.

## Quality Bar

A good report lets a reviewer answer:

- What should we do?
- Why do we believe this?
- What evidence supports it?
- What could make the recommendation wrong?
- What should a human approve before action?
